NO CLASSES ON FEBRUARY 14 DUE TO ORANGE RAINFALL

PAGASA has just issued an ORANGE RAINFALL WARNING for February 14

Schools in affected areas are expected to suspend classes. Find out if your school is included!

Understanding the Orange Rainfall Warning

The Orange Rainfall Warning is the second tier in PAGASA’s three-stage warning system for heavy rainfall. It signals that rainfall of 15-30mm per hour has been observed and is expected to continue. This level indicates an increased likelihood of flooding, particularly in low-lying areas and those near bodies of water. With the weather system becoming more unpredictable, government agencies and schools must take precautionary measures to ensure the safety of students and the general public.

Due to the persistent heavy rainfall, many local government units have opted to suspend classes in affected areas. School administrators and officials have been urged to prioritize student safety by monitoring advisories and ensuring that school communities are prepared for potential disruptions. The Department of Education (DepEd) and Commission on Higher Education (CHED) have both issued guidance regarding automatic class suspensions based on PAGASA’s alert levels.

Aside from educational institutions, businesses and local transportation services may also be impacted. Flooding in key metropolitan areas can lead to road closures, disrupted public transport routes, and even temporary power outages. Authorities advise residents to stay indoors, keep emergency supplies stocked, and remain updated through official news sources. As climate patterns shift, the increasing frequency of extreme weather highlights the importance of preparedness and responsiveness among communities and local governments.

Areas Affected

  • Metro Manila
  • Calabarzon
  • Central Luzon
  • Bicol Region
  • Western Visayas

Residents in these areas are advised to stay indoors and monitor weather updates.

What Should You Do?

If you're in an affected area, here are some safety tips:

  • Stay updated through official weather advisories.
  • Avoid wading through floodwaters to prevent infections.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, and medicine.
  • Charge your mobile devices in case of power interruptions.

Follow PAGASA and local authorities for more updates.
