NO CLASSES ON FEBRUARY 11 DUE TO ORANGE RAINFALL

PAGASA has just issued an ORANGE RAINFALL WARNING for February 11!

Schools in affected areas are expected to suspend classes. Find out if your school is included!

What is an Orange Rainfall Warning?

The Orange Rainfall Warning is the second level in PAGASA’s three-tier warning system for heavy rainfall. It indicates that intense rainfall (15-30mm per hour) has been observed and is expected to continue.

At this level, flooding is a definite possibility in low-lying areas and communities near rivers and waterways.

Why Are Classes Suspended?

When PAGASA issues an Orange Rainfall Warning, local government units and school administrators have the discretion to suspend classes to ensure students' safety.

As of now, classes have been officially suspended in several areas affected by the heavy rainfall.

Areas Affected

  • Metro Manila
  • Calabarzon
  • Central Luzon
  • Bicol Region
  • Western Visayas

Residents in these areas are advised to stay indoors and monitor weather updates.

What Should You Do?

If you're in an affected area, here are some safety tips:

  • Stay updated through official weather advisories**.
  • Avoid wading through floodwaters to prevent infections.
  • Prepare an emergency kit with food, water, and medicine.
  • Charge your mobile devices in case of power interruptions.

Stay safe and dry! Follow PAGASA and local authorities for more updates.
